About Carnation

Carnations are among the oldest cultivated flowers, admired for their frilly petals, sweet aroma, and rich symbolism. Their scientific name, Dianthus, comes from Greek words meaning "flower of the gods." The name "carnation" may come from the Latin word meaning "flesh," referring to its original pale pink color. In Christian legend, carnations are believed to have sprung from the tears of the Virgin Mary.

Types of Carnation Flowers

  • Dianthus caryophyllus
  • Dianthus chinensis
  • Dianthus ‘Everlast Lavender’
  • Dianthus furcatus
  • Dianthus giganteus
