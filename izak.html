About Carnation

Carnations are among the oldest cultivated flowers, admired for their frilly petals, sweet aroma, and rich symbolism. Their scientific name, Dianthus, comes from Greek words meaning "flower of the gods," highlighting their divine association. The common name "carnation" may derive from the Latin carnatio or incarnation, meaning "flesh," possibly referring to the original pale pink color, or from ceremonial uses in crowns and garlands. Christian legend also links carnations to the Virgin Mary, as they are said to have sprung from her tears at Jesus’ crucifixion.

Types of Carnations

  • Dianthus caryophyllus
  • Dianthus chinensis
  • Dianthus ‘Everlast Lavender
  • Dianthus furcatus
  • Dianthus giganteus
